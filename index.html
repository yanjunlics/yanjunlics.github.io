Biography

Dr. Yanjun Li is a tenure-track Assistant Professor (AI Initiative) in the Department of Medicinal Chemistry at the College of Pharmacy, University of Florida. His research interests span the fields of deep learning, drug discovery, and precision medicine, with a particular emphasis on AI-driven drug discovery (AIDD). Dr. Liâ€™s work aims to develop innovative AI algorithms to tackle foundational life science challenges with broad scientific impacts and to optimize and automate real-world drug discovery and design pipelines.

Before joining the University of Florida, Dr. Li was a Machine Learning Scientist at Calico Life Sciences, an Alphabet-founded research and development lab for human aging research, and a Senior Research Scientist at the Institute of Deep Learning, Baidu Research USA, Sunnyvale, California. He received his Ph.D. in computer science from University of Florida (2021), advised by Dr. Dapeng Oliver Wu and Dr. Xiaolin (Andy) Li. He received his dual M.E. from University of Electronic Science and Technology of China and Waseda University (2015) and B.E. from University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (2012).

Research Interests

  • Molecule Design and Optimization

    • Deep Generative Modeling for Small Molecule and RNA Design, Peptide/Antibody/Domain Protein Fix-backbone and de novo Design, etc.

  • Molecular Interaction Modeling

    • Prediction of Binding Sites, Affinity, and Poses for Various Molecular Interactions, e.g., Protein-protein, Protein-ligand, RNA-ligand, etc.

  • Precision Medicine

    • Drug Repurposing and Synergistic Combinations, Genetic Mutations Effect Prediction, Single-Cell RNA-sequencing, Spatial Transcriptomics, etc.
